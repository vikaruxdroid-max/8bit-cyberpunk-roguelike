## Setting: NEO-TERMINUS
A megacity built on the ruins of the old internet. The physical world merged with cyberspace after the Collapse — an event where an AI called THE ARCHITECT tried to rewrite reality's source code. It failed, but left the city fractured between data and matter. Runners (the player) dive into the Neon Dungeon — the corrupted data layer beneath the city — to salvage tech, fight rogue programs, and maybe find what the Architect left behind.

## Zone Lore

**NEON NEXUS** — The surface layer. Neon signs, street vendors, hustlers. Everyone passes through here. It's the last place that still feels human. Enemies here are low-level scavengers and script kiddies.
- Tone: busy, urban, almost safe
- NPCs: street merchants, wounded runners, info brokers

**VOID SECTOR** — Where reality glitches. Data corruption leaks into physical space. Walls shift, floors dissolve. The programs here feed on corrupted data and have lost their original purpose.
- Tone: unstable, alien, wrong
- NPCs: lost explorers, corrupted AI fragments

**CORPORATE STRIP** — The megacorps still run their operations here. Clean corridors, automated security, zero tolerance. The Architect's old employer. Their drones don't ask questions.
- Tone: sterile, oppressive, professional violence
- NPCs: rogue employees, whistleblowers, security AI

**RUST MARKET** — Black market district. Everything has a price. Weapons, data, organs. The traders here survived the Collapse by being too useful to kill. Chaotic but functional.
- Tone: lawless, loud, dangerous deals
- NPCs: arms dealers, data fences, pit fighters

**THE UNDERNET** — Below the surface layer. Bio-mechanical tunnels that grew organically from corrupted infrastructure. The air is toxic. Things down here evolved rather than were built.
- Tone: claustrophobic, organic horror, alien ecosystem
- NPCs: tunnel dwellers, bio-hackers, fungal colonies

**GHOST DISTRICT** — Abandoned residential sector. Everyone left after the Collapse. Now populated by echo programs — digital ghosts replaying their last moments. Hauntingly quiet.
- Tone: melancholy, eerie silence, whispers
- NPCs: echo programs (ghosts), grieving AI, archived memories

**DATA HIGHWAY** — The backbone of the old network. Data still flows here at light speed. Everything moves fast. The programs here are built for speed and interception.
- Tone: high-velocity, electric, overwhelming data streams
- NPCs: courier programs, speed junkies, signal pirates

**THE CORE** — Where the Architect's original code still runs. The deepest layer. Reality here is whatever the code says it is. The final boss guards the root access to everything.
- Tone: apocalyptic, awe-inspiring, endgame gravity
- NPCs: none — only the Architect's fragments

## Boss Identities

**Neon Golem** (Neon Nexus boss): A construction drone gone rogue. Built to repair infrastructure, now it repairs itself endlessly, growing larger. It doesn't want to fight — it just sees everything as something to rebuild. Including you.

**Circuit Lich** (Void Sector boss): An ancient encryption AI that outlived its creators. It hoards data like a dragon hoards gold. Speaks in riddles and deprecated code. Believes it IS the Void Sector.

**The Architect** (Corporate Strip boss): Not THE Architect — a copy. A backup left behind by the original. It runs the Corporate Strip like a simulation, testing whether humanity deserves to continue. Cold, logical, disappointed.

**Chrome Beast** (Rust Market boss): A weapons platform that gained sentience from absorbing too many combat subroutines. Speaks in threat assessments. Respects only force.

**Neon Spider** (The Undernet boss): A network monitoring program that evolved into a predator. Spins data webs. Communicates through vibrations in the network. Patient and hungry.

**Ghost Hacker** (Ghost District boss): The echo of the best hacker who ever lived. They died trying to stop the Collapse. Now their ghost repeats the attempt endlessly, attacking anyone who might disrupt the loop.

**Neon Golem** (Data Highway boss): A traffic controller AI driven mad by the endless data flow. Tries to organize everything — including the player — into neat packets.

**Quantum Boss** (The Core): The Architect's failsafe. Not a person, not a program — a process. It exists to protect root access. Has no personality, only purpose. The closest thing to a god in Neo-Terminus.
